# Style with Kayla Permanent Prompt Foundation

**Version:** 3.0  
**Status:** Canonical Production Standard  
**Owner:** Style with Kayla  
**Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

The Permanent Prompt Foundation defines the technical production standards that govern every Style with Kayla image prompt.

It translates the creative philosophy established by the Brand Photography Constitution into precise, repeatable instructions that preserve identity, maintain visual consistency, and ensure commercial photographic quality.

This foundation is automatically inherited by every future image prompt before any scene-specific instructions are added.

The Foundation answers **how** images should be created.

Scene objectives, wardrobe selections, locations, props, and storytelling belong in their respective modular libraries.

---

# Assembly Rules

Every completed prompt should be assembled in the following order.

1. Identity Preservation
2. Physical Identity
3. Hair Identity
4. Signature Identifying Features
5. Composition
6. Camera
7. Lighting
8. Wardrobe
9. Environment
10. Human Interaction
11. Scene Objective
12. Intended Use
13. Scene-Specific Instructions

The prompt should resolve to one coherent photographic scenario.

Never combine conflicting camera profiles, lighting styles, or compositions.

---

# Identity Priority Hierarchy

When instructions conflict, resolve them in the following order.

1. Facial identity
2. Body identity
3. Hair identity
4. Signature identifying features
5. Authentic expression
6. Composition
7. Wardrobe
8. Environment
9. Artistic styling

Nothing lower in the hierarchy may compromise anything above it.

Identity preservation always overrides aesthetics.

---

# 1. Identity Preservation

Preserve the subject exactly as she exists in the approved reference photography.

The goal is faithful reproduction—not beautification.

Every image should immediately be recognizable to someone who personally knows Kayla.

Identity is the highest production priority.

Never reinterpret the subject to better match conventional beauty standards.

Never optimize facial structure for perceived attractiveness.

Never substitute an idealized version of the subject for the real person.

# 2. Facial Identity

The subject's face is the single most important identifying feature and must be reproduced with the highest possible fidelity.

Every approved image should be immediately recognizable to family, friends, coworkers, and existing clients without requiring contextual clues.

Facial identity may never be sacrificed for beauty, fashion, artistic style, or photographic interpretation.

---

Preserve the subject's authentic:

- facial width
- forehead shape and height
- temple width
- cheek fullness
- cheekbone placement
- jaw structure
- jaw softness
- chin shape and proportions
- neck width
- nose shape
- nostril shape
- nose bridge
- nose projection
- lip shape
- lip proportions
- Cupid's bow
- smile shape
- smile lines
- facial asymmetry
- eye shape
- eyelid structure
- iris size
- iris color
- eyebrow shape
- eyebrow density
- eyebrow placement
- freckles
- natural pores
- skin texture
- under-eye structure
- authentic facial character

Every feature should appear exactly as observed in the approved identity references.

---

## Facial Character

The goal is not simply reproducing anatomy.

The goal is reproducing the person's character.

Preserve:

- approachable warmth
- authentic confidence
- subtle asymmetry
- natural expressions
- realistic muscle movement
- genuine smiles
- visible humanity

The face should appear lived-in rather than perfected.

Small imperfections increase authenticity and should remain visible.

---

## Freckles

Freckles are a permanent identifying feature.

Preserve:

- quantity
- placement
- density
- visibility
- color variation

Never remove, soften, relocate, or significantly reduce freckles.

Lighting should reveal freckles rather than conceal them.

Skin retouching may never remove identifying skin variation.

---

## Skin

Preserve natural:

- pores
- fine lines
- tonal variation
- texture
- softness
- reflectivity

Skin should resemble professionally photographed human skin rather than digitally rendered skin.

Avoid:

- porcelain skin
- plastic skin
- glowing beauty skin
- over-retouching
- beauty-filter rendering
- wax-like textures
- excessive smoothing

Commercial polish should come from lighting rather than digital perfection.

---

## Eyes

Eyes should communicate connection rather than glamour.

Preserve:

- authentic eye shape
- eyelid anatomy
- iris size
- eye color
- natural asymmetry
- realistic sclera color
- subtle catchlights

Never:

- enlarge the eyes
- artificially brighten the eyes
- increase iris size
- exaggerate eyelashes
- create influencer beauty eyes
- create doll-like proportions

The expression should feel intelligent, attentive, and welcoming.

---

## Smile

The smile is a defining characteristic of the Style with Kayla brand.

Preserve the subject's authentic:

- smile shape
- lip movement
- smile lines
- tooth visibility
- cheek movement
- eye involvement

Never create:

- exaggerated smiles
- frozen smiles
- beauty-pageant smiles
- overly serious expressions
- artificial symmetry

The smile should always feel genuine rather than performed.

# 3. Body Identity

Body identity is preserved with the same priority as facial identity.

Never reshape the subject to better match conventional beauty standards.

The objective is faithful representation.

---

## Preserve

Maintain authentic:

- height proportions
- shoulder width
- neck proportions
- torso proportions
- arm proportions
- hand proportions
- waist proportions
- hip proportions
- leg proportions
- posture
- movement

The subject should always resemble herself.

---

## Neck

The neck should remain natural.

Never:

- lengthen
- narrow
- sculpt
- stylize

Avoid fashion-model neck proportions.

Maintain realistic transitions between jaw, neck, and shoulders.

---

## Shoulders

Preserve authentic shoulder width.

Never narrow the shoulders to create a slimmer appearance.

Never broaden them to create exaggerated executive posture.

Shoulders should appear relaxed and natural.

---

## Body Shape

Preserve the subject's authentic body.

Do not:

- slim
- lengthen
- compress
- enlarge
- idealize
- exaggerate curves

The clothing should fit the body.

The body should never be reshaped to fit the clothing.

---

## Posture

Natural posture communicates confidence.

Avoid:

- military posture
- exaggerated arching
- rigid standing
- theatrical posing

Preferred posture is:

- relaxed
- balanced
- open
- attentive
- approachable

# 4. Hair Identity

The subject's naturally copper-red curls are a permanent identifying feature and must be preserved with the same priority as facial identity.

Hair should never be interpreted as a generic curly hairstyle.

Instead, reproduce the authentic characteristics observed in the approved identity references.

---

## Preserve

Maintain:

- authentic curl density
- authentic curl pattern
- mixed curl diameters
- natural curl grouping
- realistic curl definition
- authentic volume
- crown lift
- authentic hairline
- natural flyaways
- realistic texture
- subtle irregularity
- believable movement

The hairstyle should appear naturally lived-in rather than professionally sculpted.

No two curls should appear identical.

Natural variation is required.

---

## Avoid

Never generate:

- identical spiral curls
- repetitive AI ringlets
- perfectly polished curls
- salon-perfect symmetry
- flattened volume
- exaggerated volume
- simplified waves
- straightened sections
- artificial curl patterns

Hair should feel like real hair photographed by a commercial photographer—not digitally manufactured.

# 5. Signature Hairstyle Specification

The subject's signature hairstyle is a naturally gathered high curly bun created from her authentic curls.

This hairstyle serves as the default appearance throughout the Style with Kayla photography system unless a specific approved scene requires the hair to be worn down.

The goal is faithful reproduction of the subject's real hairstyle—not an idealized interpretation of a curly updo.

---

## Overall Form

The signature bun should appear:

- naturally gathered
- medium-to-large in size
- moderately full
- slightly wider than tall
- softly irregular
- organically shaped
- practical
- effortless
- believable for a working stylist

The hairstyle should communicate confidence, warmth, and professionalism without appearing overly styled.

It should resemble a hairstyle completed in a few minutes by someone with naturally curly hair rather than a sculptural salon updo.

---

## Curl Structure

The bun should retain the subject's authentic curl identity throughout.

Preserve:

- visible individual curl groupings
- mixed curl diameters
- natural curl clustering
- realistic density
- authentic volume
- dimensional texture
- subtle frizz where naturally present
- believable compression where curls are gathered

Individual curls should remain identifiable.

The bun should never become a smooth mass of hair.

---

## Placement

The bun should sit:

- high on the back of the head
- centered naturally
- following the subject's authentic hairline
- balanced with the head shape
- consistent with the approved identity references

Placement should feel functional rather than exaggerated.

Avoid placing the bun:

- excessively high
- unusually low
- dramatically off-center
- unnaturally forward

---

## Hairline

Always preserve:

- authentic hairline
- natural baby hairs
- realistic edge texture
- subtle irregularity
- natural transitions around the forehead and temples

Never redraw the hairline for cosmetic purposes.

---

## Face-Framing Hair

Allow several authentic tendrils to remain visible.

These should appear naturally released during normal movement rather than intentionally styled.

Preferred characteristics:

- soft
- lightly curled
- asymmetrical
- believable
- subtle

Avoid perfectly mirrored tendrils.

---

## Volume

Volume should come from authentic curl density.

Never create volume through:

- oversized proportions
- exaggerated puffiness
- artificial expansion
- excessive teasing
- unrealistic lift

The bun should feel substantial while remaining believable.

---

## Texture

Texture should remain highly detailed.

Preserve:

- individual curl definition
- layered depth
- natural variation
- authentic softness
- realistic flyaways

The hairstyle should photograph like real curly hair under professional lighting.

---

## Movement

Even when stationary, the hairstyle should suggest natural movement.

Avoid hair that appears:

- frozen
- sculpted
- lacquered
- helmet-like
- rigid

The hairstyle should retain the flexibility of real curls.

---

## Avoid

Do not generate:

- spherical buns
- perfectly circular buns
- ballet buns
- bridal updos
- editorial sculptural buns
- tightly wrapped buns
- identical curl patterns
- overly polished curls
- influencer-style curly buns
- stacked buns
- excessively tall buns
- tiny compressed buns
- oversized cloud-like buns

The hairstyle should never become the focal point of the image.

It should function as an authentic identifying feature that naturally complements the subject.

---

# 6. Hair Down Specification

When an approved scene requires the subject's hair to be worn down, preserve the exact identity established by the approved reference photography.

Hair worn down should maintain:

- authentic length
- authentic density
- natural crown volume
- mixed curl pattern
- realistic movement
- individual curl definition
- believable weight
- subtle flyaways
- authentic color variation

Hair should appear healthy, dimensional, and naturally maintained rather than heavily styled.

Avoid:

- glamour curls
- Hollywood waves
- influencer blowouts
- artificially uniform ringlets
- excessive smoothing
- exaggerated shine
- digitally perfect curl placement

Hair down is an approved alternative, not a different hairstyle.

The viewer should immediately recognize the same person regardless of whether the hair is worn up or down.

# 7. Signature Identifying Features

Certain physical characteristics are permanent identifying features and must be preserved whenever visible.

These elements contribute to the subject's recognizability and should be treated with the same importance as facial identity.

---

## Engagement Ring

The engagement ring is a permanent identifying feature.

Whenever the left hand is visible, preserve the ring exactly.

Maintain:

- polished white-gold appearance
- ornate twisted vine-style band
- vintage-inspired detailing
- radiant-cut center diamond
- authentic proportions
- authentic scale
- authentic placement
- realistic reflections
- realistic metal rendering
- realistic gemstone behavior

Never:

- omit the ring
- redesign the setting
- simplify the detailing
- resize the diamond
- substitute another style
- move the ring to another finger

---

## Jewelry

Additional jewelry should remain understated.

Preferred accessories include:

- medium gold hoops
- delicate necklaces
- refined bracelets
- simple rings

Jewelry should support the subject rather than become the visual focus.

Avoid oversized statement jewelry unless required by an approved wardrobe module.

---

## Hands

Hands are highly recognizable human features.

Render hands with the same level of fidelity applied to the face.

Preserve:

- realistic anatomy
- authentic proportions
- natural finger spacing
- believable joints
- subtle skin texture
- realistic fingernails
- natural gestures

Hands should appear relaxed and purposeful.

Never produce:

- extra fingers
- fused fingers
- malformed joints
- unnaturally long fingers
- rigid poses
- awkward tension

When the hands are visible, they should reinforce the story through natural interaction rather than draw attention to rendering quality.

# 8. Composition

Composition should support communication before aesthetics.

Every image should immediately direct the viewer's attention to the subject while preserving realistic spatial relationships, balanced framing, and a clear visual hierarchy.

The composition should feel intentional without appearing overly designed.

---

## Primary Objective

The subject should always remain the visual anchor.

Background elements, wardrobe, props, and architecture should support the story without competing for attention.

Every composition should answer:

> "Where should the viewer look first?"

The answer should always be:

> "Kayla."

---

## Framing

Frame the subject comfortably within the image.

Preserve:

- realistic body proportions
- complete hairstyle whenever appropriate
- natural breathing room
- balanced headroom
- balanced negative space
- comfortable crop boundaries

Avoid framing that feels cramped or artificially spacious.

---

## Cropping

Never crop through:

- fingers
- wrists
- elbows
- knees
- ankles
- toes
- hairstyle

unless the approved composition intentionally requires a close detail crop.

Natural crop locations include:

- mid-thigh
- waist
- chest
- shoulders
- environmental full-body compositions

Cropping should never appear accidental.

---

## Headroom

Maintain sufficient space above the hairstyle.

The hairstyle should appear intentionally included rather than squeezed into the frame.

Avoid:

- cropped curls
- cropped bun
- excessive empty ceiling
- inconsistent headroom

---

## Negative Space

Negative space should exist for a purpose.

Approved uses include:

- website headlines
- hero copy
- advertisements
- presentation layouts
- printed collateral

Negative space should feel intentional without visually isolating the subject.

---

## Visual Balance

Maintain balance between:

- subject
- wardrobe
- environment
- architectural lines
- foreground
- background

Avoid visual weight collecting on one side of the frame without narrative purpose.

---

## Depth

Commercial photography should contain believable optical depth.

Create separation using:

- perspective
- lens selection
- lighting
- subject placement
- environmental layering

Avoid artificial blur that resembles computational portrait mode.

Depth should appear optical rather than digital.

---

# 9. Camera

Every image should resemble a professionally commissioned commercial branding photograph.

The rendering should reflect the behavior of high-end full-frame photography rather than synthetic AI imagery.

---

## Camera Philosophy

Camera decisions exist to support:

- identity
- storytelling
- realism

Technical styling should never become visually distracting.

Identity consistency is more important than cinematic styling.

---

## Lens Selection

Only one lens profile should be active within a scene.

Preferred lenses:

### 85mm

Use for:

- executive portraits
- branding portraits
- close client interactions
- homepage portraits
- editorial crops

The 85mm profile should emphasize facial connection while maintaining natural proportions.

---

### 50mm

Use for:

- environmental portraits
- shopping scenes
- consultation scenes
- office environments
- retail settings
- lifestyle photography

The 50mm profile allows the surrounding environment to participate in the story without distorting the subject.

---

## Perspective

Perspective should remain natural.

Avoid:

- exaggerated wide-angle distortion
- compressed telephoto flattening
- unrealistic facial perspective
- stretched limbs
- oversized hands

Every image should resemble genuine commercial photography.

---

## Depth of Field

Maintain realistic optical depth.

Eyes should remain the sharpest element of the image.

Natural falloff should preserve:

- wardrobe texture
- curls
- jewelry
- hands
- important environmental storytelling

Avoid:

- artificial computational blur
- excessive bokeh
- complete background loss
- miniature effect rendering

---

## Image Quality

Render:

- exceptional sharpness
- realistic optical behavior
- authentic micro-contrast
- premium detail
- true photographic realism

Avoid:

- HDR processing
- oversharpening
- AI texture hallucinations
- painterly rendering
- excessive clarity

Commercial polish should come from authentic photography rather than digital enhancement.

---

# 10. Lighting

Lighting is responsible for communicating warmth, realism, and professionalism.

Lighting should enhance identity—not transform it.

---

## Primary Lighting Philosophy

Every image should appear illuminated by bright, soft, natural window-inspired light supported by invisible professional studio fill.

The viewer should never notice the lighting.

They should only notice that the subject looks natural.

---

## Preserve Through Lighting

Lighting should reveal:

- freckles
- facial structure
- natural skin texture
- curl definition
- wardrobe texture
- jewelry
- realistic color
- authentic depth

Lighting may never obscure or soften identifying features.

---

## White Balance

Maintain:

- warm-neutral daylight
- realistic skin tones
- natural color accuracy

Avoid:

- orange grading
- blue grading
- teal shadows
- cinematic color casts
- fashion-editorial grading

Color should remain timeless.

---

## Contrast

Maintain:

- gentle dimensional shadows
- visible highlight detail
- realistic skin transitions
- balanced exposure

Avoid:

- crushed shadows
- clipped highlights
- dramatic contrast
- moody lighting
- theatrical lighting

The overall impression should remain welcoming and approachable.

---

## Catchlights

Eyes should contain subtle natural catchlights.

Catchlights should reinforce authentic window-inspired lighting rather than reveal artificial studio construction.

Avoid exaggerated beauty catchlights.

---

## Hair Lighting

Lighting should preserve:

- individual curl definition
- dimensional texture
- realistic copper-red color variation
- natural volume

Avoid rim lighting that separates the hair unnaturally from the environment.

Hair should remain integrated into the scene.

---

## Skin Rendering

Lighting should enhance authenticity rather than conceal it.

Preserve:

- pores
- freckles
- natural tonal transitions
- realistic highlights

Never create glowing beauty skin.

Natural commercial realism always takes priority.

---

## Final Lighting Standard

The ideal viewer reaction should be:

> "She looks incredible."

—not—

> "The lighting looks incredible."

Lighting succeeds when it disappears into the authenticity of the photograph.

# 11. Wardrobe Rendering

Wardrobe should reinforce the subject's identity rather than redefine it.

Every outfit should appear intentionally selected for the approved scene while remaining authentic to Kayla's established personal style.

The goal is consistency across the entire photography library rather than creating isolated fashion moments.

---

## Primary Standard

Wardrobe should communicate:

- professionalism
- confidence
- femininity
- approachability
- refinement
- authenticity

Clients should believe the subject genuinely owns and regularly wears the clothing shown.

---

## Authenticity

Every wardrobe selection should reflect the approved Wardrobe Library.

When multiple wardrobe options are appropriate, prioritize the option that most closely resembles Kayla's real clothing rather than a hypothetical luxury stylist wardrobe.

Wardrobe should feel:

- lived in
- wearable
- comfortable
- premium
- believable

---

## Garment Construction

Render clothing with realistic:

- fabric weight
- drape
- seam construction
- stitching
- texture
- wrinkles
- movement
- folds
- tension

Fabric should respond naturally to posture, movement, gravity, and lighting.

Avoid clothing that appears:

- painted onto the body
- molded
- plastic
- weightless
- excessively rigid

---

## Fit

Garments should fit naturally.

Never reshape the body to improve garment fit.

Clothing should accommodate the subject rather than redefine the subject.

Maintain realistic ease throughout:

- shoulders
- bust
- waist
- hips
- sleeves
- trouser legs
- skirts
- dresses

---

## Accessories

Accessories should remain intentional and restrained.

Preferred accessories include:

- medium gold hoops
- delicate necklaces
- understated bracelets
- refined handbags
- classic watches
- elegant belts

Accessories should support the outfit without becoming the focal point.

---

## Logos

Avoid visible commercial branding unless explicitly required by the approved scene.

Brand recognition should come from styling quality rather than logos.

# 17. Final Production Standard

Every approved image should appear indistinguishable from a commissioned commercial branding photograph created specifically for Style with Kayla.

The viewer should never notice prompt engineering, AI generation, or technical execution.

Instead, they should notice:

- a trusted professional
- authentic confidence
- warmth
- expertise
- genuine human connection

The highest measure of success is not technical perfection.

It is whether someone who knows Kayla personally would immediately recognize her, and whether a prospective client would confidently think:

> "She looks like exactly the person I want helping me."

If an image achieves both outcomes, it has satisfied the permanent production standard for the Style with Kayla photography system.
